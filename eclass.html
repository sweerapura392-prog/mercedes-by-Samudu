Generations & Highlights

Ponton & Fintail (W120/W121/W110, 1953–1968)

Monocoque bodies, improved safety “Ponton” design, and “Fintail” models introducing early crumple zones and directional stability — the template for mid-size Mercedes.

W114/W115 (1968–1976)

Modernized suspension and packaging, reliable four- and six-cylinder engines, taxis and private owners loved the durability.

W123 (1976–1985)

Legend of longevity. Offered sedan, coupe, and wagon (T-Modell). Advanced safety for the time; became a global workhorse.

W124 (1985–1995)

Aerodynamic, refined ride, multi-link rear suspension, 4MATIC AWD, and high-performance 500E/E500 collaboration era.

W210 (1995–2003)

Distinctive twin-oval headlights, greater efficiency, ESP stability control rollout, and popular diesel variants.

W211 (2002–2009)

Further safety, SBC (early electro-hydraulic braking on some years), improved interiors, and AMG E55/E63 excitement.

W212 (2009–2016)

Sharper lines, advanced driver assistance, and a comprehensive facelift adding cohesive design and MBUX precursors.

W213 (2016–2023)

Tech-rich: widescreen cockpit, semi-autonomy, refined diesels and turbo petrols, AMG E53/E63 performance spectrum.

W214 (2023–present)

Latest digital architecture, electrified powertrains, and enhanced comfort — bridging tradition and modern UX/UI.
