The Beginning of Mercedes-Benz & Its Founders

The story starts with Karl Benz, who patented the three-wheeled Benz Patent-Motorwagen in 1886, widely regarded as the first practical automobile. In parallel, Gottlieb Daimler and engineer Wilhelm Maybach developed high-speed petrol engines and early motorcars. In 1926, Benz & Cie. and Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft merged to form Daimler-Benz AG, and the unified passenger cars adopted the name Mercedes-Benz — “Mercedes” coming from DMG’s successful racing and customer cars named after Emil Jellinek’s daughter, Mercédès.
