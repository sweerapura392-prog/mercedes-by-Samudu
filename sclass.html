Generations & Key Years

W116 (1972–1979)

The first officially designated S-Class. Pioneered advanced safety with crumple zones, fuel tank above the rear axle, and (later) introduced ABS in collaboration with Bosch. High refinement, powerful inline-six and V8 engines.

W126 (1979–1991)

Global luxury benchmark for a decade. Aerodynamic design, extensive rust protection, widespread adoption of airbags, seatbelt pretensioners, and improved fuel economy. Long-wheelbase SEL variants popular among dignitaries.

W140 (1991–1998)

Nicknamed the “Tank” for vault-like build. Double-glazed windows, soft-close doors, optional V12, advanced traction control, and early telematics. Set a new bar for isolation and ride comfort.

W220 (1998–2005)

Lighter and sleeker than W140. Debuted COMAND infotainment, DISTRONIC radar cruise, AIRMATIC air suspension, and innovative lighting. Expanded multimedia and ergonomic advances.

W221 (2005–2013)

Sharper chassis, higher active-safety integration: Night View Assist, advanced PRE-SAFE, and improved semi-active suspension. Widely praised interiors and AMG performance variants.

W222 (2013–2020)

Technological tour-de-force: LED lighting standard, semi-autonomous features, Magic Body Control with road surface scan, extensive driver aids, and plug-in hybrid options. Interior luxury and ambient lighting set trends.

W223 (2020–present)

MBUX with 3D instrument display, augmented-reality navigation, expanded Level 2 driver assistance, rear-wheel steering, and E-Active Body Control (on select markets). Comfort, NVH, and tech integration are reference-grade.
